Natural Flavoring

question_mark

UNCERTAIN for your Jain Diet

The origin of this ingredient is ambiguous.

What is it?

Natural flavoring is an umbrella term used to describe flavorings derived from nature (plants, meat, seafood, dairy, etc.) rather than created synthetically. The exact source material is often proprietary and not disclosed on the label.

warning

Why is it uncertain?

Natural flavoring can be derived from various sources, including both permitted plants and prohibited items like root vegetables, alcohol, or animal products. Without specific origin certification, it is impossible to verify if it meets strict Jain dietary standards.

lightbulb

Recommendations

  • Contact the manufacturer directly to ask if the natural flavors are derived from root vegetables or animal sources.

  • Consider avoiding if strict adherence is required and the product is not certified Jain/Vegetarian.
